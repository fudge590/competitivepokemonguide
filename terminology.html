Competitive Pokemon Terminology

There are many abbreviations and terms we use in the world of competitive pokemon, and I'm here to help you understand them. Let's take a look!

Common terms

Here are some commonly used terms, separated into categories for ease of digestion. Yum!

Teambuilding

Term Description Remarks
Stat HP, Atk, Def, SpA, SpD, Speed None
Physical/Special move Damaging moves that use Atk and SpA in damage calculation Physical damage targets Def, while Special damage targets SpD
Status move Moves that apply status effects(eg. Toxic) May be both volatile or non-volatile
Priority move Damaging moves that trade power for priority(eg. Quick Attack) Speed does not matter in priority. The move with the highest priority will always go first. This goes unless both pokemon use a move with the same priority, in which case the faster pokemon will go first.
Sweeper A hard hitting, fast pokemon that uses damaging moves to continuously KO pokemon if left ignored This type of pokemon should be checked immediately when they are switched in(or even before if anticipated) to stop them from snowballing.
Tank A bulky pokemon that uses both damaging and utility moves, while being able to absorb damage This type of pokemon can defend itself against pokemon that are weak to it, but can also take neutral damage hits well. It is vulnerable against pokemon that are supereffective against it.
Wall An ultra-bulky pokemon that usually only uses utility moves, and can absorb absurd amounts of damage This type of pokemon can only stall against pokemon that are weak to it. However, it can shrug off neutral damage hits, and can take a few supereffective hits against itself.
Wallbreaker An ultra-hard hitting, usually bulky pokemon that has extremely high Atk or SpA stats, allowing it to output insane amounts of damage This type of pokemon can KO tanks and walls, but is vulnerable to other pokemon because of its low speed. Some sweepers can be wallbreakers too.
Glass cannon A hard hitting, fast pokemon with low defensive stats in return. Somewhat synonymous with sweepers. However, some sweepers trade speed in return for bulk. These type of pokemon are countered by priority moves.
Stall team A team strategy which involes slowly KO-ing the opponent's pokemon while avoiding being KO-ed in return This type of team usually consists of tanks and walls, and may use status effects to deal damage. This kind of team is countered by wallbreakers and revenge killers.
Pivot team A team strategy which involves using moves like U-turn and Volt Switch, using type resistances to recieve damage efficiently This type of team usually consists of fast pokemon who can switch safely. This kind of team is countered by entry hazards and good prediction.
Hyper offensive team A team strategy which involves hard hitting, fast sweepers whose end goal is to KO your pokemon as fast as possible This type of team usually consists of a full team of sweepers that require little to no set-up, together with a suicide lead. The goal of the suicide lead is to set up entry hazards, whether it dies or not is irrelevant. This kind of team is countered by walls and sweepers.
Balanced team A team strategy which involves a balance of all types of pokemon This type of team usually consists of a wide type coverage and type resistances. However, the team does not excel at anything, and may find it hard to function if one or two key pokemon are knocked out.

Pokemonshowdown Commands

All commands have the prefix "!" or "/"
! makes the information show up on both sides / makes the information only visible to you

Command Description Remarks
data [pokemon-name]/[ability-name]/[move-name]/[item-name] Shows you data about what you want. If it is a pokemon, shows you the base stats I use this frequently to estimate damage
weak [pokemon-name]/[type] Shows you the weakness and resistances of a pokemon Useful if you still have to refer to type weakness chart
coverage [type]/[move] Shows you what the move is good and bad against Basically like /weak, but the other way round
